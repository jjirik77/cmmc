Case Study Instructions

Module: CMMC-AUD-101

Your Role

You are a provisional CMMC Assessor. You have been hired to perform a Mock CMMC Level 2 Assessment for AeroTech Dynamics, a small manufacturer of drone guidance fins for the DoD.

The Objective

AeroTech believes they are ready for certification. Your job is to review the evidence provided in the "Evidence Repository" tabs and verify if they actually meet the NIST 800-171 requirements.

The Deliverable

You must complete the Security Assessment Report (SAR) located in the bottom tab. You need to:

  1. Review the evidence.
  2. Hunt for inconsistencies between what they say (Policy) and what they do (Logs/Interviews).
  3. Determine if controls are MET or NOT MET.
  4. Write the Remediation Recommendations for the CEO.

Interview Transcript: IT Administrator

Subject: Marcus T. (IT Lead)
Date: Oct 12, 2024
Assessor: Let's talk about Access Control (AC.L2-3.1.1). How do you manage administrative accounts?
Marcus (IT): Well, we have a 'Domain Admin' account. Since it's just me and the junior tech, Sarah, we usually just share the password for that account to keep things moving fast. It's stored in a KeePass vault we both can open.
Assessor: I see. And what about Multi-Factor Authentication (IA.L2-3.5.3)?
Marcus (IT): We definitely have MFA enabled for the VPN. If you work from home, you have to use the app on your phone.
Assessor: What about when you log into the Server locally in the server room? Or when you RDP internally?
Marcus (IT): Internally? No, that would slow us down too much. If we are in the building, the physical door lock is the security. We don't use MFA for local logins.
Assessor: Moving on to Maintenance (MA.L2-3.7.3). How do you handle equipment sanitization before disposal?
Marcus (IT): Oh, we have a pile of old laptops in the closet. We plan to drill the hard drives eventually, but right now they are just sitting there. The door is locked though.

Interview Transcript: HR Director

Subject: Linda K. (HR)
Date: Oct 12, 2024
Assessor: Can you describe the onboarding process regarding security screening (PS.L2-3.9.1)?
Linda (HR): Absolutely. Every employee undergoes a background check before they are hired. We check criminal history and references.
Assessor: Great. What happens when an employee is terminated or leaves voluntarily? specifically regarding asset return and account disabling (PS.L2-3.9.2)?
Linda (HR): We do an exit interview on their last day. They hand in their badge. I send an email to IT usually by the end of the week to let them know the person is gone so they can close the account.
Assessor: Wait, you notify IT "by the end of the week"? So if someone is fired on Monday, IT might not know until Friday?
Linda (HR): Usually, yes. Marcus is busy, and I assume if they don't have a badge, they can't get in anyway, right?

Security Assessment Report (SAR)

Instructions: Based on the evidence in the other tabs, complete this report. Look for conflicts between Policies (what they say) and Configs/Interviews (what they do).

1. Executive Summary

Summarize the overall risk posture. Is AeroTech ready for certification? What are the critical blockers?

2. Scope & Environment

Define the boundary. What is IN scope and what is OUT? Refer to the SSP.

3. Detailed Findings (The Hunt)

Analyze the specific controls below based on the evidence found.
AC.L2-3.1.1 (Account Management)

Limit system access to authorized users/processes acting on their behalf.

AC.L2-3.1.8 (Unsuccessful Login Attempts)

Limit unsuccessful login attempts (Lockout).

SC.L2-3.13.1 (Boundary Protection)

Monitor, control, and protect organizational communications (Firewalls).

PS.L2-3.9.2 (Personnel Termination)

Ensure systems access is removed upon termination.

4. Risk Analysis & Recommendations

Which "NOT MET" finding poses the greatest immediate threat to CUI?
What specifically must AeroTech do to fix the issues identified above?

5. Conclusion & Attestation
